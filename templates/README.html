Solar Sizing Tool Documentation

Overview

The Solar Sizing Tool is a web-based application designed to help users determine the required components for an off-grid or grid-tied solar PV system. By entering load details, system parameters, and environmental factors, the tool calculates battery capacity, PV array size, inverter requirements, and charge controller specifications. The results are presented clearly, with options to export or share.

New Features and Improvements

  • Dynamic Input Interface
  • Automatic System Voltage Determination
  • Days of Autonomy and Tilt Angle
  • Inverter Settings (Efficiency & Type)
  • Module Efficiency Adjustment (80%)
  • Adjustment Factor for Real-World Conditions
  • Automatic Sizing for Modules, Inverters, and Charge Controllers
  • Exportable Results (Word Document)
  • Sharing Options (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Email)

Step-by-Step Calculations (Sample Scenario)

1. Total Daily Energy Demand

Energy (Wh) = Quantity × Power (W) × Daily Hours

  • LED Lights: 4 × 10 W × 5 h = 200 Wh
  • Refrigerator: 1 × 150 W × 24 h = 3600 Wh
  • Fan: 2 × 50 W × 8 h = 800 Wh

Total Daily Energy Demand: 4600 Wh

2. Energy Requirement with Losses

Total Energy with Losses = 4600 Wh × (1 + 0.1) = 5060 Wh

3. Required Battery Capacity

Required Capacity (Ah) = 5060 ÷ (12 × 0.5 × 0.8) ≈ 1054.17 Ah
Chosen Battery Size: 6 × 200 Ah = 1200 Ah

4. Module Sizing

Total Module Size (Wp) = 5060 ÷ 5 = 1012 Wp
Chosen Configuration: 6 modules of 200 Wp

5. Charge Controller Sizing

Controller Current = (1012 W ÷ 12 V) × 1.25 ≈ 106 A
